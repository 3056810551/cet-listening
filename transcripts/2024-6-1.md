W: Yes, "The History of Jazz" by Ted Gioia is also very popular. It's more academic, but it has a lot of detailed information about different jazz styles and musicians.

M: Yes, here it is.

M: Thank you. I appreciate your help.

The participants were divided into two groups. One group engaged in moderate exercise, such as walking or swimming, for at least 30 minutes a day, three days a week. The other group did not engage in any regular exercise.
At the end of the five-year period, the researchers found that the exercise group had better memory, attention, and problem-solving skills than the non-exercise group. They also had a lower risk of developing dementia.
The researchers explain that exercise increases blood flow to the brain, which helps to nourish brain cells and promote the growth of new neurons. It also reduces inflammation and oxidative stress, which are both associated with cognitive decline.
The study suggests that regular exercise is a simple and effective way to maintain brain health as we age. Even moderate exercise, such as walking, can have significant benefits.

## PASSAGE 2

The Internet of Things (IoT) is a network of physical devices, vehicles, home appliances, and other items embedded with electronics, software, sensors, and connectivity, which enables these objects to connect and exchange data.
The IoT has the potential to transform many aspects of our lives. For example, smart homes can automatically adjust the temperature, turn on the lights, and lock the doors based on our preferences. Smart cars can communicate with each other and with traffic lights to reduce accidents and improve traffic flow.
However, the IoT also raises some concerns. One of the main concerns is security. As more devices become connected to the Internet, they become vulnerable to hacking and cyberattacks. Hackers could potentially gain access to our personal information, control our devices, or even cause physical harm.
Another concern is privacy. The IoT generates a huge amount of data about our daily lives. This data could be used by companies to target us with advertising, or by governments to monitor our activities.
To address these concerns, we need to develop better security measures and privacy laws to protect users. We also need to educate people about the risks of the IoT and how to protect themselves.

## RECORDING 1

Climate change is one of the most pressing issues facing the world today. It is caused by the release of greenhouse gases, such as carbon dioxide, into the atmosphere. These gases trap heat from the sun, causing the Earth's temperature to rise.
The effects of climate change are already being felt around the world. We are seeing more frequent and severe heatwaves, droughts, floods, and storms. These extreme weather events can cause significant damage to property, infrastructure, and agriculture, and can also lead to loss of life.
Climate change also has a negative impact on human health. It can increase the spread of infectious diseases, such as malaria and dengue fever, as mosquitoes and other disease-carrying insects expand their range. It can also cause respiratory problems due to air pollution and wildfires.
To address climate change, we need to reduce our greenhouse gas emissions. This includes transitioning to renewable energy sources, such as solar and wind power, improving energy efficiency, and reducing our reliance on fossil fuels. We also need to adapt to the changes that are already happening, such as building sea walls to protect against rising sea levels and developing drought-resistant crops.
In addition, we need to raise awareness about climate change and encourage people to take action. Everyone can do their part by reducing their carbon footprint, such as driving less, using energy-efficient appliances, and eating less meat.

To ensure that AI is used for the benefit of humanity, we need to develop ethical guidelines and regulations for its use. We also need to invest in education and training to help people adapt to the changing job market.

Reading is a fundamental skill that is essential for success in life. It not only provides us with knowledge and information, but also helps us develop critical thinking, empathy, and imagination.
Reading exposes us to different ideas, cultures, and perspectives. It allows us to learn about the experiences of people from all over the world and from different time periods. This helps us develop empathy and understanding for others.
Reading also helps us develop critical thinking skills. When we read, we have to analyze and evaluate the information we are presented with. We have to distinguish between fact and opinion, and identify the author's purpose and point of view.
In addition, reading can improve our vocabulary, grammar, and writing skills. It exposes us to different writing styles and helps us learn how to express ourselves more effectively.
Unfortunately, many people today do not read as much as they used to. With the rise of social media and digital technology, people are spending more time watching videos and scrolling through social media feeds than reading books.
To encourage more people to read, we need to make reading more accessible and enjoyable. This includes providing access to books in schools, libraries, and communities, and promoting reading as a fun and rewarding activity.
